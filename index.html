About Me

I am a junior studying computer science and philosophy at the University of Maryland, College Park in the class of 2024. My primary interests are in artificial intelligence, specifically deep learning and natural language processing. I believe some of my strengths are adaptability and ability to work collaboratively.

I am most interested in machine learning because it broke all my notions on how computers work and how to code. I always thought computers were just machines that do math quickly but are incapable of doing anything if not ordered to. But machine learning flipped that notion on its head, not only can models can learn patterns and trends adjust themselves following the patterns to make the most informed decisions possible.

Currently, I am working on a couple of projects in machine learning. One group of them is, making my implementation of different models, I have completed a single-layered perceptron and plan on working on a K-nearest neighbor algorithm next. The goal of the previous projects is to gain a better understanding of machine learning so I can begin working on my long-term project of developing a chess engine.
